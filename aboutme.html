Inspiration for the Website

MY STORY

Through the curriculum at Babson College, I was able to expand some of my skills and learn how to code using HTML and Javascript. As a deliverable for the class, I was given the task to create a website about anything of my choosing. I knew that I wanted to create a website about how to teach yourself how to play guitar - it is something I did about 2 Â½ years ago during COVID. The purpose of this website is to synthesize the information and materials I found useful over the process of teaching myself how to play and I want to prove that you don't need a fancy guitar or private lessons to learn. In addition, this site will include my personal advice, challenges, progress, and next steps. Enjoy 🎸!

During my sophomore year of college, we were all sent home due to COVID-19 and I quickly found myself practicing a monotonous routine. I think like everyone, I started to spend a lot of time watching YouTube videos. One day, I came across a video of John Mayer and Keith Urban playing "Don't Let Me Down" and was amazed. I did some research into the possibility of teaching myself how to play guitar out of curiosity. I have very limited background in music. To my surprise, I found out that many great guitarists taught themselves how to play (including Keith Urban).

My family owned an old guitar that had been in my basement for years. I looked up how to tune it, and I was all ready to go. The only problem was I had no idea where to start. The amount of information was quite overwhelming.

I found an app called "Yousician" where I learned the basic chords and how to strum, as well as some basic guitar theory. After I got the chords down, I was hooked. Soon I was learning full songs and taking requests - fast forward to today where I can learn practically anything I want with time and patience.
